Mr. Squidward’s League Constitution
*Amendments for each season at the bottom*

Welcome to our FUN league! By participating, you agree to abide by the following rules and guidelines. Any concerns or issues regarding these rules must be raised; failure to do so implies your acceptance of them. All matters not explicitly addressed will be resolved at the discretion of the commissioner, whose decisions are final. Our aim is to foster a fun, fair, and competitive environment, and we expect your cooperation and sportsmanship throughout the season.

## Article I – League Governance

### 1. Commissioner Role
- The founding commissioner is established, with subsequent commissioners to be elected through a vote by league members.
- The commissioner is the primary authority and handles disputes, rule violations, and overall league management.
- The commissioner’s decisions are final unless otherwise specified by a super majority (2/3rds) vote of league members.

### 2. Voting
- Major rule changes or decisions can be put to a vote.
- Each team will have one vote.
- Commish gets two if there is a tie breaker.
- Super majority (2/3rds) vote of league members required.

## Article II – Team and Roster Management

### 1. Team Size
- Number of teams: 16
- Roster spots per team: 10
- Positions: G, F, C, Flex, Bench

### 2. Daily Lineup
- 3 guards
- 3 forwards
- 2 centers
- 2 flex

### 3. Injured List (IL) Restrictions
- 2 IL slots
- Players placed on IL must be removed within one week plus the remaining days in the current period once they become healthy.
- Failure to remove healthy players from IL will result in a complete restriction from making any transactions (lineup adjustments, claims, trades, or other moves).
- Unforeseen Circumstances: If unforeseen circumstances arise, it is understood that the affected team will be at a disadvantage. Teams must manage accordingly.
- Anti-Stash Rule: Players without an injury designation (red flag) may remain on IL for a maximum of 2 full weeks. After this period, if the player is not activated or dropped, the team will be completely locked from all transactions until the roster is corrected.

## Article III – Draft

### 1. Format
- Auction draft

### 2. Budget
- $200 per team

### 3. Maximum Bid

### 4. Draft Timing
- Before the start of the season

### 5. Technical Issues (Draft)
If you miss a draft pick due to technical reasons, you must reach out to the commissioner right away.

If it is determined to be a bug, the pick will be reverted to the price and person that paid for it and the time will resume from 15 seconds.

If it was a failure due to a connection issue or not paying attention, you’re SOL.

#### 5.1 5-Second Buffer Rule
- All bids should be placed before the timer reaches 5 seconds.
- Bids placed in the final 5 seconds are risky—if a technical issue occurs, there may not be enough time to pause and resolve it.
- If you notice a display issue (wrong price, bid not registering, etc.), call it out before the timer hits 0. The commissioner can pause to investigate.
- If the timer hits 0 and the pick finalizes, reversals are much harder to justify.

#### 5.2 Types of Technical Issues
- Category A – Verifiable Platform Bugs
  - The platform itself malfunctions (e.g., bid doesn’t register despite clicking, price display freezes, nomination skipped incorrectly).
  - If multiple managers witness or experience the same issue, it’s likely a platform bug.
  - Resolution: Pick may be reverted. Timer resumes from 15 seconds.

- Category B – Individual Display/Sync Issues
  - Your screen shows a different price than what others see.
  - Bid appears to go through on your end but doesn’t register on the platform.
  - Resolution: You must call it out before the timer hits 0 so the commissioner can pause. If you have a screenshot or screen recording, share it. The commissioner will ask other managers what they saw on their screens. If multiple people corroborate the issue, the commissioner may revert. If it’s just your word with no evidence or corroboration, you’re likely SOL.

- Category C – Connection Issues
  - Your internet drops, app crashes, or you get kicked out.
  - Resolution: If you (or someone on your behalf) call(s) it out before the timer hits 0, the commissioner can pause. If the timer hits 0 and the pick finalizes before anyone calls it out, you’re SOL.

- Category D – User Error
  - You weren’t paying attention, misclicked, or misread the price.
  - Resolution: You’re SOL. The bid stands.

#### 5.3 Jump Bid Auto-Pause
- Definition: A jump bid is any bid that increases the current price by $10 or more in a single bid.
- The Problem: When a jump bid happens, managers who were clicking +$1 increments may accidentally bid over the jump before realizing the price changed.
- Auto-Pause Rule:
  - Commissioners will pause at their discretion when a significant jump bid occurs.
  - Early jumps on star players (e.g., $40 to $70) are expected and won’t trigger a pause.
  - Once bidding gets competitive and managers are in +$1 mode, a $10+ jump may trigger a pause to let people process.
  - No call-out needed—the commissioner will pause when they feel it’s warranted.
  - If you still bid after the pause, you own it. No reversals.

#### 5.4 How to Report
- Immediately message the commissioner in the draft chat or via DM.
- If possible, take a screenshot or screen recording showing the issue.
- Describe what you saw vs. what actually happened.

#### 5.5 Commissioner Review
- The commissioner will ask other managers what they saw to corroborate.
- If multiple managers confirm something looked off, the commissioner may revert.
- If it’s just your word with no evidence, you’re likely SOL.
- The commissioner’s determination is final.

#### 5.6 Mid-Draft Break
- A scheduled break will occur at the mid-point of the draft (after Round 5 or when approximately half of rosters are filled).
- Use this time for bathroom, smoke, snacks, or to stretch.
- Length will be announced at the start of the draft.

#### 5.7 Pre-Draft Tech Check
- Managers are encouraged to test their connection and platform functionality at least 15 minutes before the draft begins.
- Report any issues to the commissioner before we start.

### 6. Commissioner’s Discretion
Anything missed by these rules will be determined by the commissioner.

### 7. Draft Order
Draft order will be determined at least 5 minutes before the draft begins.

The previous season’s champion has the option to reroll the order twice if they choose to do so.

The process follows:
- Order A (initial randomization)
- Reroll B
- Reroll C

The champion must accept the final order that is generated. Once a reroll is used, they cannot revert to a previous order.

They have time to decide if they want to stick with it or reroll.

The default is to choose the 3rd one.

### 8. Matchup Schedule
The regular season matchup schedule will be determined at least 15 minutes before the draft begins.

Similar reroll rules apply as the draft order:
- The previous champion chooses how many rerolls we do then we land on the final schedule.
- Purely picking how many rerolls before we stop.
- The default is to choose the 3rd one.

### 9. Auction Timers
#### Definitions
- Nomination Timer: How long the nominating team has to name a player
- Bid Timer: How long everyone has to place a bid once a nomination is made
- Extension (Bid Reset): How much time is added after each new bid

#### Timing by Draft Phase
Phase 1 (Rounds 1–2 – Top targets)
- Nomination: 45s
- Bid: 20s
- Extension: 15s

Phase 2 (Rounds 3–6 – Core rotation players)
- Nomination: 35s
- Bid: 15s
- Extension: 10s

Phase 3 (Rounds 7–End – Endgame/fillers)
- Nomination: 25s
- Bid: 10s
- Extension: 5s

Commissioner Discretion: These are guidelines. The commissioner may adjust timers throughout the draft based on pace, energy, and vibes.

## Article VII – Trades

### 1. Trade Deadline
11:59 PM the day before playoffs start. No trades allowed post-deadline.

### 2. Missed Games Insurance
Trades can include insurance for missed games, with a maximum duration of 14 days from the day the trade is accepted by all parties.

Not when the commissioner pushes it through.

Specifics must be agreed upon and documented by all parties involved in the trade, even those that are not a part of the policy.

See Appendix for example format.

### 3. Finality of Trades
Once trades are accepted by all parties and not vetoed by the council, they are final.

Do not make trades if you are unwilling to follow through.

Trades made under the influence of alcohol or other substances are still valid.

### 4. Fairness of Trades
For those who are newer to basketball, fantasy sports or just believe they require help, the veto for trades has a lower requirement level. The commissioner or others will provide input on the advisability of trades.

### 5. Veto Council
Trades may be reviewed by a Veto Council to ensure league fairness and competitive balance. See Section 6 for council structure, process, and appeals.

### 6. Trade Council Structure & Process
#### 6.1 Purpose
The Veto Council exists to preserve league fairness and competitive balance while allowing managers freedom to make trades. Its purpose is to prevent trades that create unintentional blowouts, collusion, or clear misunderstandings of player value that disadvantage other managers, while still allowing for manager autonomy and learning.

#### 6.2 Council Structure & Roles
- Councillor A & B: Primary reviewers for all trades.
- Councillor C: Alternate member who only votes when A or B are involved in a trade.
- Commissioner: Tiebreaker vote only.

#### 6.3 Conflict of Interest Protocol
- If either Councillor A or B is involved in a trade:
  - The uninvolved primary councillor reviews.
  - Councillor C joins as the second reviewer.
  - Commissioner serves as tiebreaker.

- If both Councillor A and B are trading with each other:
  - Councillor C and the Commissioner review as a two-person panel.
  - If they disagree, a league-wide emergency vote is triggered.

#### 6.4 Council Selection & Terms
- All three council members are appointed at the start of the season.
- Terms last as long as the commissioner decides.
- Members are selected via commissioner.
- Consecutive terms are permitted, but the commissioner will aim to rotate members each season to ensure fresh perspectives.
- Mid-season replacements follow the same nomination process.
- Councillor C participates in discussions even when not voting to stay informed.

#### 6.5 Eligibility & Guidelines for Vetoes
- Trades may only be vetoed to prevent collusion or to protect a manager who clearly misunderstands player value in a way that threatens league fairness.
- Veto decisions are based on council members’ judgment, not rigid formulas.
- Managers with less experience may receive additional guidance.
- Experienced managers’ trades cannot be vetoed solely for inexperience; vetoes must be based on fairness evaluation.

#### 6.6 Yellow Flag System
Questionable trades that are not veto-worthy trigger a “yellow flag.”

Council reaches out to manager(s) for discussion/education.

Trades proceed after conversation unless the manager voluntarily withdraws.

#### 6.7 Council Process
- All trades are submitted to the council for review.
- Trades involving top X players require additional documentation from both managers (see Section 10).
- Active council members evaluate fairness based on player value, projections, and league impact.
- Review Timeline: Trades are reviewed within a few hours of submission (24 hours for trades near roster lock deadlines).
- Commissioner breaks tie decisions.

#### 6.8 Communication & Transparency
- Veto decisions are communicated clearly to the league.
- General reasoning is shared to build understanding of trade standards while protecting manager privacy.
- Council decisions are final; their purpose is maintaining fairness, not personal preference.
- When possible, council will post brief context or approval notes after a trade to help set the tone before reactions spiral.

#### 6.9 Appeals Process
- Managers may submit one appeal within 12 hours of a veto notification.
- Appeals must provide new information or context not initially considered.
- Full council plus Commissioner reviews appeals; majority vote decides.
- Appeal decisions are final with no further review.

### 7. No Player Rentals/Temporary Trades
All trades must be intended as permanent roster moves.

Prohibited: “Lending” or “renting” players between teams for temporary periods with the intent to trade back.

This includes: Trades made to help a team meet minimum game requirements, win a specific matchup, or any other temporary roster need.

Penalty: Immediate veto and potential loss of trading privileges.

Clarification: Any agreement (written or implied) to reverse a trade constitutes collusion.

### 8. Proper Roster Management Reminders
Teams struggling to meet 25-game minimums should:
- Use IR spots for injured players
- Drop underperforming players for streaming options
- Make legitimate trades for permanent roster improvements
- Accept the L if unable to field sufficient games

### 9. New Manager Trade Review Period
Applies to: Managers with two (2) seasons or fewer in the league.

Rule: During the first six (6) weeks of the season, all trades involving a new manager must go through the Trade Council for guidance before execution.

When to Involve Council: Council review is required once both managers have agreed in principle and the trade is close to being finalized. Exploratory conversations and negotiations do not require council involvement.

Purpose: Education and guidance, not blocking trades. After six weeks, new managers trade normally under standard council review.

Post-Eligibility: Once a manager has more than two full seasons, trades are entirely on them drunk or not.

Conflict of Interest: If a council member is involved, standard hierarchy protocols apply.

### 10. Top X Player Trade Oversight
Definition: Top five (5) players in the league by FP/G at the time the trade is proposed.

Timing Clarification: Rankings are evaluated at the moment the trade is submitted to the council. If a player falls out of the top 5 before a trade is proposed, this rule does not apply. If a player enters the top 5 after a trade is already submitted, the original evaluation stands.

When to Involve Council: Council review is required once both managers have agreed in principle and the trade is close to being finalized. Exploratory conversations and negotiations do not require council involvement.

Requirements:
- Council review required.
- Both managers must privately explain to the council why they believe the trade is fair.
- Council may suggest adjustments if the trade appears unbalanced.

Optional League-Wide Veto:
- After council approval, top X trades may be subject to league-wide vote.
- Veto requires 66% of non-involved managers (11 of 16) voting “no.”
- Voting window is 24 hours.

### 11. Trade Withdrawal (“Cold Feet Rule”)
Either manager may withdraw a trade at any time before final execution no explanation required.

Withdrawn trades may be revised and resubmitted. The new trade must go through the full council review process again.

Submitting a trade to council does not lock you in. If council approves and you get cold feet, you can still back out. The trade only becomes final once both managers confirm after council approval.

Repeated or excessive withdrawals by the same manager may be flagged by council for discussion.

### 12. Trade Discussion Etiquette
- Critique the trade, not the manager.
- Allow trades time to digest before reacting first impressions are emotional, not analytical.
- Council-approved trades have met fairness standards, even if you personally disagree.
- If you wouldn’t say it to their face at the draft, don’t type it in the chat.

### 13. Trade Visibility and Reactions
Trade notifications are instant via the app and Facebook. There is no way to delay, censor, or buffer trade announcements.

Managers should give themselves time before reacting publicly. A trade that looks crazy at first glance often makes more sense after studying both rosters.

When possible, council will post brief context or approval notes after a trade to help set the tone before reactions spiral.

## Article XI – Prizes

### 1. Champion Prize
Trophy, bragging rights, and the right to choose next year’s draft order if they want.

### 2. Consolation Tournament
The teams not in the top 8 (bottom 8) will participate in a consolation tournament. The winner of the consolation tournament will receive $10–$15 towards next year’s draft.

### 3. Consolation Tournament Loser Punishment
The team that finishes in last place in the consolation tournament will be required to wear a “loser” jersey during the next draft or must provide a social media post about their defeat.

### 4. Best Record Bonus
The team with the best overall record at the end of the regular season will receive a bonus waiver wire priority at the start of the next season. Let the commissioner know.

### 5. Toilet Bowl Lottery System
Teams finishing in the bottom 8 of the regular season participate in the Toilet Bowl tournament. The winner of the lottery receives $10 in bonus auction cash for the following season.

Lottery odds are determined by a weighted point system based on regular season finish and Toilet Bowl tournament results.

#### Total Lottery Points
Each team’s Regular Season Finish Points and Toilet Bowl Tournament Points are added together to determine their total lottery weight.

#### Non-Returning Managers
If a manager is not returning for the following season, their lottery points are removed from the total pool. Remaining eligible teams’ odds adjust proportionally.

#### Lottery Timing
The Toilet Bowl Lottery will be conducted on draft night before the draft begins for all seasons going forward.

Lottery odds will be calculated based only on confirmed returning managers at the time of the draft.

The $10 bonus auction cash will be awarded to the lottery winner for use in the current draft immediately following the lottery drawing.

### 2. Additional Rules for Insurance
- The default for insurance trades is that the manager with a policy gets to choose if the policy is used if it activates. They do not automatically get their player(s) back it must be said so. It can be discussed in terms that it does happen automatically.
- If it was a multi-team trade, the policy will only apply to the managers accepting the policy.
- You may trade players already under an insurance policy, but you must say so to the new manager and they must know the risk that the player may be reverted back to its original owner.
- If two policies are activated, only one manager needs to decide to use it for the trade to be reversed. No canceling out the other’s policy by using your own (like burns in president).
